## Logical and ($\land$)
$p \land q$
- is TRUE when BOTH $p$ and $q$ are TRUE.
- is FALSE when at least one of the premises is FALSE.

## Logical or ($\lor$)
$p \lor q$
- is TRUE when at least one of them are TRUE.
- is FALSE when both are FALSE.

## Conditional statement ($\to$)
$p \to q$
- is FALSE when $p$ is TRUE and $q$ is FALSE.
- TRUE otherwise.

### Tips
- $\neg(p \to q) \equiv p \land (\neg q)$
- $p \to q \equiv \neg q \to \neg p$

## Biconditonal statement ($\leftrightarrow$)
$$
	p \leftrightarrow q \equiv (p \to q) \land (q \to p)
$$

known as **if and only if** = **iff**

## De Morgans's law
$$
	\neg (p \lor q) \equiv (\neg q) \land (\neg q)
$$
$$
	\neg (p \land q) \equiv (\neg q) \lor (\neg q)
$$

## Quantifiers
- $\forall x(P(x) \to (Q(x)) \equiv$ "for all $x$, if $P(x)$ then $Q(x)$".
- $\exists x(P(x) \land Q(x)) \equiv$ "there exists an $x$ such that $P(x)$ and $Q(x)$".
